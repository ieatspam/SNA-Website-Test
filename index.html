    Hands-On Activities

    Try these fun exercises to apply what you’ve learned!

    • Build Your Network: Use string and paper cutouts to map out your classroom's network. Who are your friends? Who talks to whom?
    • Drag & Drop Game: Match SNA terms (like "node" and "edge") to pictures or emojis representing them.
    • Centrality Chase: Pretend you're the most connected node—how fast can you pass a message through the network?

    Quiz Yourself!

    Ready to test your SNA knowledge?

    1. What do we call the connections between people in a network?
    2. What does a "node" represent?
    3. What is a cluster?
    4. What is a bridge node and why is it important?
    5. What does "degree" tell you in a network?
    6. What’s an example of a community in your life?
    7. What might happen if a bridge node leaves a network?

    Answers: 1. Edges 2. A person or thing 3. A tightly-knit group 4. A connector between groups 5. Number of connections 6. Class, team, family, etc. 7. The network may split
